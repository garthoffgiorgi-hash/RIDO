Four ways to set the name
Same type (Sora), same Midnight, same Signal accent, same layout. Only case and the accent change.
Lowercase · accent
rido
Current. Warm, modern, conversational — matches "get a rido." The accent is the signature.
Lowercase · all midnight
rido
Quiet. Friendly but more grown-up. Reads clean and timeless; loses the ownable spark.
Uppercase · accent
RIDO
Bold. Authority plus energy — the accent keeps caps from feeling corporate. Strong app-icon material.
Uppercase · all midnight
RIDO
Solid. Most "established," most trustworthy, least playful. Monolithic and serious.
What you're actually choosing between
Case → temperature
Lowercase reads warm, approachable, peer-to-peer (fits the UCSD crowd). Uppercase reads solid and trustworthy — useful when you need riders to feel safe getting in a car.
Accent → ownership
The Signal "i" is the one thing that's uniquely yours and ties to the brand's whole "signal" idea. Dropping it is cleaner and more timeless, but more anonymous — closer to a hundred other blue wordmarks.
