# RIDO — Business Overview

*Context doc. The one-page "what are we even building." Synthesized from our strategy work; see siblings for depth.*

## What it is
A driver-favorable rideshare marketplace in the Uber/Lyft category. Riders request rides, drivers fulfill them, RIDO runs the platform and takes a cut. Two-sided marketplace; **driver supply is the hard side.**

## The wedge (the whole reason RIDO exists)
Incumbents take a large, opaque cut of each fare — nominally ~25%, but **effectively ~35–50%** since "upfront pricing" (2022) decoupled rider price from driver pay. Drivers resent it. RIDO takes far less, so **drivers keep more and riders pay a fairer price at the same time.** The only thing standing between those two facts is how much the middle takes.

## The mission (the soul, not the headline)
Founder's conviction: Uber/Lyft are predatory monopolies that underpay drivers — one face of a broader problem of work that no longer sustains people. RIDO is proof a ride can be cheaper *and* fairer. **Message hierarchy: lead with the tangible (cheaper, safe, sleek); fairness right behind; the movement is the resonant why, surfaced for those who lean in — never the hero.** A fairness brand makes its promise a constraint: build economics that keep the promise keepable.

## Who it's for
- **Riders:** first wedge is the UCSD nightlife crowd (party rides, the 2am ride home); app is for any ride. They convert on price/safety/reliability; the mission drives loyalty and word-of-mouth.
- **Drivers:** students themselves + local Uber/Lyft drivers fed up with the take. Interviews confirm some would pay a $150–200/mo fee and still prefer RIDO. Drivers are the hero RIDO fights *for*.

## Beachhead
**San Diego / UCSD.** Dense, concentrated geography with predictable demand (nightlife, airport runs, campus) — good for the cold-start problem. California regulates TNCs at the state level (CPUC), a cleaner path than the city-by-city regulators that destroyed Empower.

## Status
Pre-launch, capital-constrained, first-time founder (technically strong, newer to the business side). Monetization model is finalized in direction (see `monetization-model.md`); brand is established (see `../brand/`); database architecture is designed (see `technical-architecture.md`).

## The standing strategic lenses (pressure-test everything against these)
Liquidity/cold-start · per-city geographic density · unit economics at real utilization · driver-supply economics (who actually benefits) · regulation/insurance/classification as *viability*, not paperwork · incumbent response (they have near-infinite capital).
